CARDS TO AVOID (By Clan)

Mardu (RWB) Weak Cards

  • Desperate Measures - Unreliable card draw, too situational
  • Strategic Betrayal - Situational exile effect, often dead card
  • Bearer of Glory - Overcosted pump spell with minimal impact

Jeskai (URW) Weak Cards

  • Fresh Start - Temporary debuff that doesn't affect board state
  • Spectral Denial - Situational counter that's often too narrow
  • Ringing Strike Mastery - Expensive removal that costs too much for effect

Sultai (BGU) Weak Cards

  • Winternight Stories - Overcosted draw spell with minimal impact
  • Strategic Betrayal - Situational exile that's often dead in hand
  • Auroral Procession - Weak recursion effect for the cost

Abzan (WBG) Weak Cards

  • Smile at Death - Overcosted recursion with unreliable timing
  • United Battlefront - Unreliable permanent selection, often whiffs
  • Rebellious Strike - Weak combat trick that rarely matters

Temur (GUR) Weak Cards

  • Roamer's Routine - Basic land search that's too slow
  • Summit Intimidator - Overcosted reach creature with poor stats
  • Snowmelt Stag - Inconsistent stats for the cost

General Tips for Identifying Weak Cards

  • ❌ Overcosted effects (paying too much for the impact)
  • ❌ Highly situational spells that are often dead draws
  • ❌ Creatures with poor stats-to-cost ratio
  • ❌ Cards that don't affect the board immediately
  • ❌ "Win more" cards that only work when you're already ahead

Quick Reference Guide

Mana Base Guidelines

  • 17-18 lands for a 40-card deck
  • Stick to 2 colors with light splash of third if necessary
  • Prioritize dual/tri lands and mana fixing artifacts
  • For 3-color decks: minimum 3 sources of each color

Card Evaluation Tips

  • Prioritize 2-for-1 value cards (draw + effect)
  • Premium removal is top priority
  • Evasive creatures (flying, trample) are strong
  • Value card advantage effects
  • Avoid cards with too many specific conditions

Deck Building Shortcuts

  • 15-17 creatures
  • 6-8 pieces of interaction
  • 2-3 answers to large creatures
  • 2-3 answers to flyers
  • Most creatures should cost 2-4 mana

Clan-Specific Tips

  • Mardu (RWB): Aggressive curve, prioritize early drops
  • Jeskai (URW): Balance spells and creatures
  • Sultai (BGU): Don't overload on graveyard cards
  • Abzan (WBG): Focus on creature quality over quantity
  • Temur (GUR): Watch your mana requirements carefully

In-Game Tips

  • Keep hands with 2-4 lands
  • Mulligan hands with no plays before turn 3
  • Save removal for key threats
  • Don't overcommit against board wipes
  • Be mindful of combat tricks

Sealed Pool Organization

  • Sort by color first
  • Then by mana cost
  • Separate creatures and non-creatures
  • Keep removal spells visible
  • Set aside unplayable cards immediately

Between-Match Adjustments

  • Track what works/doesn't work
  • Consider sideboarding against specific strategies
  • Don't be afraid to change colors if needed
  • You can modify your deck between matches

Set Mechanics

  • Mobilize - When this creature attacks, create tapped and attacking Warrior tokens
  • Renew - Exile from graveyard for powerful effects
  • Flurry - Triggers on casting your second spell each turn
  • Endure - Put counters or create Spirit tokens
  • Dragon Tribal - Many cards care about Dragons or become stronger with Dragons
  • Behold - Choose a Dragon you control or reveal a Dragon card from hand

Event Rules

  • 40-card minimum deck size
  • All cards opened can be used (including multiple copies)
  • Basic lands are provided by the store
  • You can change your deck between rounds
  • Match time limit is usually 50 minutes
  • Call a judge if you have any questions!
  • Best-of-three matches with sideboarding

Phase Reminders

  • Untap → Upkeep (triggers) → Draw
  • Main 1 → Combat → Main 2
  • Combat steps: Begin → Attackers → Blockers → Damage → End
  • End Step → Cleanup (discard to 7)
  • Remember to announce all triggers!

Prerelease Etiquette

  • Keep your cards organized and visible
  • Announce all your plays clearly
  • Be friendly and helpful to new players
  • Maintain a clean play area
  • If in doubt, call a judge - it's what they're there for!
  • Take reasonable time for decisions
  • Shuffle opponent's deck after they shuffle
  • Present deck to opponent for cut before each game
